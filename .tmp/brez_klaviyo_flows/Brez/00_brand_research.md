# Brand Research: Brez

### 1. BRAND FOUNDATION
**Brēz was founded in 2023 by Aaron Nosbisch, a 31-year-old serial entrepreneur from Western Illinois who started his first business at age 13 selling t-shirts online and later built ventures in e-commerce, cannabis, and wellness, including a CBD brand and an aromatherapy vape line.**[1][3][7] Nosbisch launched Brēz on April 20 (420), bootstrapping with under $20,000 after recognizing a gap for fast-acting, low-dose THC beverages as a social, alcohol-free alternative; the brand achieved profitability in six months, $1.3 million in year-one revenue, $28-29 million in 2024, and $13 million in Q1 2025.[1][2][3][7] **Mission centers on redefining social drinking with clarity, wellness, and intentional living, providing an enjoyable cannabis experience without alcohol's downsides like hangovers or unpredictability of edibles/inhalation.**[2][4][5] Values emphasize connection, curiosity, mindfulness, and functional benefits like improved cognition via Lion’s Mane mushrooms; positioning as "everyone's favorite social lubricant" and "healthier alternative to social drinking."[4][5] **Unique selling propositions (USPs) include low-dose THC for anxiety-free dosing, fast-acting beverage format, Lion’s Mane for health perks (cognition, inflammation), and expansion to mixable spirits/shots for versatility.**[1][3][5] Brand voice is relatable, inspiring, and premium—rooted in Nosbisch's personal story of turning cannabis passion into profit, with a timeless "ancient-future" aesthetic blending wellness and social fun.[2][6]

### 2. PRODUCT PORTFOLIO
**Main categories: Low-dose THC-infused functional beverages (tonics), cannabis spirits (750ml bottles for mixing/sipping), and portable THC shots for on-the-go use; recent non-cannabis drinks added.**[1][3][7] **Hero products are the original low-dose tonics infused with Lion’s Mane mushrooms, best-sellers driving rapid scaling as America's top cannabis beverage by revenue.**[1][2] Price points position as premium yet accessible (specifics not detailed, but supports $28M+ revenue via DTC and retail like Sprouts), targeting alcohol-replacement market.[2][5] **Key ingredients/features: THC (low-dose for controlled effects), Lion’s Mane mushrooms (cognition, anti-inflammation), crafted for fast onset, mixability in cocktails, and social enjoyment without alcohol's negatives; developed with bartenders like Lynette Marrero for cocktail performance.**[1][3][4][6]

### 3. TARGET AUDIENCE
**Primary demographics: Millennials/Gen Z (Nosbisch's generation, 25-40s), wellness-focused adults in the US, including social drinkers shifting from alcohol; based in Palm Beach County, FL, appealing to urban/suburban professionals.**[2][5][7] **Psychographics/lifestyle: Intentional livers prioritizing clarity, mindfulness, and functional wellness over hangovers; cannabis enthusiasts seeking social, non-inhalable formats; community-oriented, curious about "ancient-future" innovation.**[2][4][5] **Pain points: Unpredictable edibles, inhalation aversion, alcohol downsides (anxiety, inflammation, regret); advertising roadblocks in cannabis; desire for profitable, feel-good socializing without negatives.**[1][4][5] **Purchase motivations: Alcohol alternative for social occasions, reliable dosing for confidence, health boosts (cognition via Lion’s Mane), premium experience in growing non-alcoholic trend.**[2][4][5]

### 4. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
**Key competitors: Other THC beverages/edibles in the fast-growing cannabis drink sector (e.g., implied rivals Brēz outpaces); broader functional/non-alcoholic drinks like moderation wines (e.g., Maggie Frerejean-Taittinger) or culture-first brands (e.g., Madre).** [1][2][6] **Differentiation: Outpaces rivals in revenue/speed ($28M year one); low-dose, Lion’s Mane-infused for cognition/wellness; beverage format beats edibles/inhalation on speed/dosing; bartender-tested mixability; DTC scaling with retail push (Sprouts).**[1][3][5][6] **Market positioning: Premium leader in THC functional beverages as social lubricant/healthier alcohol swap, riding non-alcoholic wellness wave with strong brand architecture and community focus.**[2][4][5][6]

### 5. EMAIL MARKETING INSIGHTS
**Resonant messaging: "Elevate your social hour without the hangover—clarity, connection, and a better buzz"; lean on founder story ("From backyard joints to $28M empire") and "social lubricant" tagline for relatable aspiration.**[1][4][5] Position as intuitive upgrade: "Fast-acting, low-dose bliss in a sip—ditch unpredictable highs and alcohol regrets."[5] **Key benefits to highlight: Low-dose THC + Lion’s Mane for sharp cognition and reduced inflammation; mixable versatility (tonics, spirits, shots); profitability-proven (e.g., "$13M Q1 2025") as trusted bestseller; alcohol-free clarity for intentional living.**[1][2][4] **Objections to address: Dosing fears ("Anxiety-free, precise low-dose"); legality/access ("DTC + retail like Sprouts"); taste/mixability ("Bartender-crafted for cocktails"); cannabis stigma ("Healthier social alternative, not your old weed").**[3][4][5] Use social proof: "Outpacing competitors—join 50M revenue wave."[2] **Emotional triggers: Belonging/connection ("Gather with friends, feel good after"); empowerment/transformation ("From stressed to clear-headed—Nosbisch's journey"); FOMO/exclusivity ("420 launch legend, now mainstream"); affirmation/relief ("Wife-approved wellness, no downsides").**[1][4][5] For campaigns: Welcome series with founder backstory; abandoned cart with "Pocket-ready shot for spontaneous vibes"; loyalty with "Unlock mixology recipes"; urgency via limited drops.[2][3]
