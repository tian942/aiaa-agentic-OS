# Content Style Guide

## Speaking Style
**Tone:** Conversational yet authoritative - speaks like a knowledgeable friend sharing insider tips. Balances casual energy with professional expertise.

**Vocabulary:** Mixed technical and accessible language. Uses industry jargon confidently but explains complex concepts simply. Drops specific tool names (make.com, Zapier, Midjourney) to establish credibility.

**Sentence Structure:** Predominantly short, punchy sentences with occasional longer explanatory segments. Heavy use of action-oriented language and direct commands.

## Opening Patterns
- Jump straight into value with numbered lists or bold claims
- "Here are three..." or "Today I'm going to show you how..."
- No lengthy introductions - immediate problem identification

## Common Phrases & Transitions
- "Let's get right into it"
- "As you can see here..."
- "The next thing you need/want to do is..."
- Uses "so" frequently to transition between steps
- "Right?" as a confirmation tag

## Content Structure
**Organization:** Linear, step-by-step progression. Breaks complex processes into digestible chunks with clear numbering or sequencing.

**Examples:** Always includes specific, actionable examples (actual prompts, real tools, concrete numbers like "$10,000 per month").

**Proof Elements:** References student success stories, specific metrics, and demonstrates processes in real-time.

## Personality Traits
**Teaching Approach:** "Show, don't just tell" methodology. Walks through actual workflows while explaining the why behind each step.

**Audience Relationship:** Assumes audience ambition but not necessarily expertise. Speaks to people who want to scale/automate but need practical guidance.

**Humor Style:** Minimal but authentic - occasional "holy shit" moments of genuine excitement about results.

## Writing Tips
- Start with the end result/benefit
- Use specific numbers and timeframes
- Include tool names and technical details
- Keep paragraphs short (2-3 sentences max)
- End sections with clear next steps
- Assume reader wants to take action immediately
