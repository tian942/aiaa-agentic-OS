# Brand Research: Brez

### 1. BRAND FOUNDATION
**Brez was founded in 2023 by Aaron Nosbisch, a 31-year-old serial entrepreneur from Western Illinois who started his first business at age 13 selling t-shirts online and later built ventures in e-commerce, cannabis, and wellness, including a CBD brand and an aromatherapy vape line.**[1][3][7] Nosbisch launched Brez on April 20 (cannabis "high holiday") after identifying a gap for fast-acting, low-dose THC beverages as a social, alcohol-free alternative, drawing from personal experiences with cannabis and challenges scaling prior projects with minimal capital (under $20,000).[1][3][5] The company achieved $1.3 million in first-year revenue, $28-28.9 million in 2024, and $13 million in Q1 2025, becoming profitable within six months while outpacing competitors.[1][2][7]

**Mission centers on redefining social drinking with clarity, wellness, and intentional living, offering enjoyable cannabis experiences without alcohol's downsides like hangovers or unpredictability of edibles/inhalation.**[2][4][5] Values emphasize connection, curiosity, mindfulness, and functional benefits (e.g., Lion’s Mane for cognition and inflammation reduction), rooted in Nosbisch's Florida-based life with his wife Skye.[1][4][5] **Positioning as "Everyone’s Favorite Social Lubricant" and "healthier alternative to social drinking," blending ancient-future aesthetics for premium, mainstream appeal in DTC and retail like Sprouts.**[4][5]

**Unique selling propositions (USPs) include low-dose THC for anxiety-free dosing, fast-acting beverage format, Lion’s Mane infusion for cognitive health, and versatility (tonics, spirits, portable shots for mixing/sipping).** [1][3][4][5] **Brand voice is bold, relatable, and aspirational—conversational founder storytelling mixed with premium wellness vibes, evoking confidence, fun, and elevation without regret.**[2][4][5]

### 2. PRODUCT PORTFOLIO
**Main categories: Low-dose THC-infused beverages (tonics, 750ml spirits for mixing/pouring, portable shots), with expansions into non-cannabis options; crafted with input from bartenders like Lynette Marrero for cocktail performance.**[1][3][4]

**Best-sellers/hero products: Original low-dose tonics infused with Lion’s Mane mushrooms, positioned as the core functional social drink; spirits and shots as flexible extensions for on-the-go or restaurant use.**[1][3][4]

**Price points: Premium positioning (not specified exactly, but supports $28M+ scaling via DTC and retail, implying $10-30/can or equivalent for spirits/shots based on functional cannabis beverage norms).** [2][5]

**Key ingredients/features: THC (low-dose for predictable effects), Lion’s Mane mushrooms (cognition, anti-inflammation), designed for fast onset, social enjoyment, no hangover, and mixability; "ancient-future" formulation for wellness without traditional cannabis downsides.**[1][4][5]

### 3. TARGET AUDIENCE
**Primary demographics: Millennials/Gen Z (20s-30s, like founder), urban/suburban wellness seekers in legal cannabis states, including social drinkers shifting from alcohol; appeals to Florida/Palm Beach crowd and national retail shoppers.**[2][5][7]

**Psychographics/lifestyle: Intentional livers prioritizing clarity, mindfulness, and sober-curious social experiences; cannabis enthusiasts avoiding smoking/edibles' unpredictability; health-focused (e.g., anti-inflammation, cognition), community-oriented, open to premium functional beverages.**[2][4][5]

**Pain points/desires: Alcohol's hangovers/regret, inconsistent edibles/high anxiety, inhalation aversion; desire for reliable buzz, social lubrication, cognitive boost, and feel-good nights without consequences.**[4][5]

**Purchase motivations: Healthier partying, wellness integration, convenience (portable/dosable), FOMO on viral growth ($50M trajectory), and cultural shift to non-alcoholic alternatives.**[2][5]

### 4. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
**Key competitors: Other THC beverages (unspecified but noted as outpacing "nearly every other"); broader functional/non-alc like Madre (culture-first), premium NA wines (Maggie Frerejean-Taittinger), emphasizing Brez's cannabis edge.[1][6]

**Differentiation factors: Superior scaling speed ($28M Year 1), low-dose precision + Lion’s Mane for functional benefits, bartender-tested mixability, DTC-first with retail push, founder-led authenticity vs. edibles/smoking.**[1][3][5]

**Market positioning: Premium leader in THC beverages as alcohol replacement—faster, cleaner, social; mainstream via Sprouts while rooted in cannabis culture, ahead of category growth.**[1][2][5]

### 5. EMAIL MARKETING INSIGHTS
**Resonant messaging: "Elevate your night without the crash—Brez: clarity meets buzz" or founder stories like "From backyard joints to $28M: Join the shift"; lean on social proof (revenue milestones, profitability).[1][2][4]

**Key benefits to highlight: 
- Hangover-free social high with precise low-dose THC.
- Lion’s Mane for sharper focus and reduced inflammation.
- Versatile (mix, sip, shot) for any vibe—home, bar, events.
- Fast-acting, anxiety-free alternative to alcohol/edibles.**[1][4][5]

**Objections to address: 
- Dosing fears: "Predictable low-dose, every time—no paranoia."
- Legality/access: "Legal states, DTC shipping + retail like Sprouts."
- Taste/effects: "Bartender-approved for cocktails; feel good, not wrecked."
- Novelty skepticism: "Proven: $28M Year 1, outpacing rivals."**[1][3][5]

**Emotional triggers: 
- **Freedom/relief**: Escape alcohol guilt, embrace sober-curious joy.
- **Aspiration**: Join wellness elite (clarity, intention, connection).
- **FOMO/urgency**: "Don't miss the fastest-growing THC bev—stock up."
- **Community**: "Social lubricant for real bonds, like founder’s journey."**[2][4][5]

**Actionable email tactics: Segmented flows—welcome series with Nosbisch backstory/ab tests; abandoned cart with "One sip to clarity"; re-engagement via Q1 2025 revenue wins; UGC from events for trust.**[3][5]
