# SKILL BIBLE: VSL RECORDING WITH OBS STUDIO

## Executive Summary

This skill bible provides a comprehensive framework for creating professional Video Sales Letters (VSLs) using OBS Studio. A VSL is a conversion-focused video asset designed to present service offerings, establish credibility, and drive prospects to book calls or take action. Unlike casual content creation, VSL recording requires strategic planning, professional presentation, and technical precision to effectively convert high-value B2B prospects.

The methodology emphasizes quality over speed, with 60% of effort invested in research and setup, and 40% in execution. This approach recognizes that well-produced VSLs significantly outperform rushed alternatives in conversion rates. The process covers everything from creative brief development and script writing to advanced OBS configuration and post-production workflows, ensuring every VSL serves as a professional sales asset that works 24/7.

This skill transforms basic screen recording into broadcast-quality video production, differentiating practitioners from competitors using simple tools like Loom. The comprehensive approach builds credibility with sophisticated buyers while maintaining authentic delivery that connects with prospects on a human level.

## Core Principles

1. **Quality Over Speed Philosophy**: Invest 60% of time in research and setup, 40% in execution. Well-produced VSLs convert significantly better than rushed ones, making thorough preparation essential for success.

2. **Authenticity Trumps Perfection**: While technical quality matters, authentic delivery builds more trust than perfect production. Be human, show personality, and connect genuinely with prospects.

3. **Professional Differentiation**: OBS-recorded VSLs immediately separate you from competitors using basic screen recording tools, signaling expertise and building credibility with high-value prospects.

4. **Strategic Asset Mindset**: VSLs are not content but strategic sales assets designed to work continuously on landing pages, requiring the same level of investment as other critical business infrastructure.

5. **Data-Driven Iteration**: First VSLs won't be perfect. Record, test, gather conversion data, and continuously improve based on performance metrics rather than assumptions.

6. **Congruency Across Touchpoints**: VSL messaging must align perfectly with ad copy, landing page content, and confirmation pages to maintain prospect trust and maximize conversion rates.

7. **Technical Excellence Enables Message**: Superior audio/video quality removes barriers to message reception, allowing prospects to focus on value proposition rather than production issues.

8. **Preparation Prevents Poor Performance**: Comprehensive creative briefs, rehearsed scripts, and tested technical setups eliminate variables that could derail recording sessions.

## Step-by-Step Process

### Phase 1: Strategic Foundation (Days 1-3)

**Step 1: Creative Brief Development**
1. Define target avatar with precision:
   - Decision maker role and authority level
   - Company size (revenue/employee range)
   - Current situation and pain points
   - Desired outcomes and success metrics
   - Likely objections and concerns
2. Establish messaging hierarchy:
   - Hook that matches advertising message
   - Credibility establishment method
   - Problem agitation strategy
   - Solution introduction approach
   - Proof stack organization
   - Process breakdown structure
   - Objection handling sequence
   - Call-to-action specifics
3. Compile proof elements:
   - 3+ case studies with specific results
   - Testimonial quotes from similar clients
   - Relevant credentials and authority markers
   - Process transparency elements
4. Ensure congruency check:
   - Ad message alignment
   - Landing page copy consistency
   - Confirmation page continuity

**Step 2: Script Development**
1. Brain dump all content without structure constraints
2. Organize content into 8-part framework:
   - Introduction (30-45 seconds)
   - Problem Agitation (1-2 minutes)
   - Solution Introduction (1 minute)
   - Proof Stack (2-3 minutes)
   - Process Breakdown (1-2 minutes)
   - Objection Handling (1 minute)
   - Call to Action (30 seconds)
3. Condense to 1,200-1,500 words for 5-10 minute target
4. Conversationalize language for natural delivery
5. Rehearse 3-5 times, timing each run-through

**Step 3: Presentation Creation (Gamma.app)**
1. Sign up for Gamma.app account
2. Input script sections for AI-assisted slide generation
3. Apply design principles:
   - 5-7 words per slide maximum
   - High contrast color schemes
   - Consistent brand application
   - Generous white space utilization
4. Create slide types:
   - Title slides with value propositions
   - Problem slides with visual representations
   - Proof slides with client logos and metrics
   - Process slides with clear step sequences
   - CTA slides with specific action steps
5. Optimize for 15-20 second slide timing (10-30 slides total)

### Phase 2: Technical Setup (Day 4)

**Step 4: OBS Installation and Configuration**
1. Download OBS Studio from obsproject.com
2. Skip Auto-Configuration Wizard for manual setup
3. Configure Video Settings:
   - Base Resolution: 1920x1080
   - Output Resolution: 1920x1080
   - FPS: 30
   - Downscale Filter: Lanczos
4. Configure Output Settings:
   - Recording Format: MP4
   - Encoder: x264 or Apple VT H264
   - Rate Control: CRF at 18-23
   - CPU Preset: veryfast
5. Configure Audio Settings:
   - Sample Rate: 48 kHz
   - Channels: Stereo
   - Desktop Audio: Disabled
6. Set up hotkeys for recording control

**Step 5: Camera Setup**
1. For built-in cameras:
   - Add Video Capture Device source
   - Select FaceTime HD Camera or equivalent
   - Set highest available resolution
   - Position and crop to face area
2. For external cameras (Sony ZV-E10, Canon, etc.):
   - Connect via USB or HDMI capture card
   - Configure camera settings (Manual mode, 1/60 shutter, f/2.8-f/4 aperture)
   - Enable clean HDMI output
   - Add Video Capture Device in OBS
   - Position for professional framing

**Step 6: Audio Configuration**
1. For built-in microphones:
   - Verify Mic/Aux audio source
   - Set levels to peak -12dB to -6dB
   - Apply noise suppression filter
2. For external microphones:
   - Connect XLR mic to audio interface
   - Connect interface to computer via USB
   - Add Audio Input Capture source
   - Set to Mono in Advanced Audio Properties
   - Apply filter chain: Noise Suppression → Noise Gate → Compressor → Limiter
3. Test recording and adjust levels accordingly

### Phase 3: Scene Composition (Day 4 continued)

**Step 7: Scene Creation**
1. Create new scene named "VSL Recording"
2. Add Color Source for background (white or brand color)
3. Add Window Capture for presentation slides
4. Position slides on left side (60% of frame width)
5. Crop slides to remove browser elements
6. Add camera source on right side (40% of frame width)
7. Crop camera to focus on face and upper torso
8. Ensure no gaps or black bars in composition

**Step 8: Presentation Integration**
1. Open Gamma.app presentation in browser
2. Enter Present/Full-Screen mode
3. Configure Window Capture in OBS to detect presentation
4. Fine-tune positioning to show only presentation content
5. Test slide advancement to ensure smooth transitions

**Step 9: Lighting and Environment Setup**
1. Position key light at 45° angle to face
2. Add fill light on opposite side at 50-75% intensity
3. Optional: Add hair light behind for separation
4. Set all lights to 5000K-5600K color temperature
5. Test camera white balance and exposure
6. Prepare quiet recording environment

### Phase 4: Recording Execution (Day 5)

**Step 10: Pre-Recording Preparation**
1. Complete 24-hour checklist (script finalization, technical testing)
2. Complete 1-hour checklist (hydration, vocal warm-up, mental preparation)
3. Complete 15-minute checklist (lighting, notifications, final tests)
4. Complete 5-minute checklist (positioning, breathing, mindset)

**Step 11: Recording Process**
1. Perform final OBS check (preview, audio levels, focus)
2. Start recording with slate information
3. Begin VSL delivery with energy 10-20% above conversation level
4. Advance slides every 15-30 seconds coordinated with script
5. Maintain eye contact with camera lens
6. Use natural gestures and authentic delivery
7. Complete full run-through without stopping for minor mistakes

**Step 12: Recording Management**
1. Record multiple takes if needed (Take 1, Take 2, etc.)
2. Slate each take for organization
3. Note any issues or improvements between takes
4. Stop recording and save files with descriptive names

### Phase 5: Post-Recording Workflow (Day 6)

**Step 13: Immediate Review**
1. Watch recording immediately while fresh in memory
2. Note timestamps of any issues or excellent moments
3. Evaluate overall performance, energy, and message clarity
4. Decide if additional takes are needed

**Step 14: File Organization**
1. Create project folder structure
2. Move recordings to appropriate folders
3. Backup files to cloud storage
4. Name files with clear conventions (ProjectName_VSL_Take1_Date)

**Step 15: Basic Post-Production (Optional)**
1. Trim beginning slate and end dead time
2. Adjust audio levels if needed
3. Color correct if necessary
4. Export final version for upload
5. Create multiple formats if required (different resolutions, etc.)

## Best Practices

### Pre-Production Excellence

**Creative Brief Depth**: Spend 60% of total project time on creative brief development. Most VSL failures stem from unclear messaging, not technical issues. Know your avatar's exact pain points, desired outcomes, and likely objections before writing a single word.

**Script Rehearsal Importance**: Rehearse scripts 3-5 times before recording. This isn't memorization but familiarity building. You should know the flow well enough to sound natural while staying on message.

**Congruency Maintenance**: Ensure perfect alignment between ad copy, VSL hook, landing page messaging, and confirmation page content. Any disconnect breaks trust and reduces conversion rates.

**Proof Stack Specificity**: Use specific, measurable results in case studies. "Increased revenue by 40%" is better than "helped grow their business." Include timeframes and company context for credibility.

### Technical Production Standards

**Audio Priority**: Invest in audio quality before video quality. Prospects will tolerate lower video resolution but will abandon videos with poor audio. Clean, professional audio signals competence and builds trust.

**Lighting Consistency**: Use artificial lighting rather than natural light for consistent results across multiple recording sessions. Color temperature should remain constant at 5000K-5600K for professional appearance.

**Camera Positioning**: Position camera at eye level or slightly above. Never shoot from below (unflattering) or too far above (diminishing). Eyes should be in upper third of frame with slight headroom.

**Scene Composition Balance**: Allocate 60% of frame width to slides and 40% to camera. This prioritizes content while maintaining human connection. Avoid centering yourself (slides need space) or making camera too small (loses personal connection).

### Recording Performance Optimization

**Energy Calibration**: Speak with 10-20% more energy than normal conversation. Cameras absorb energy, and recorded content feels less energetic than live delivery. Compensate accordingly while maintaining authenticity.

**Pace Management**: Speak slightly slower than conversation pace. Allow important points to land with brief pauses. Don't rush through content—prospects need processing time for complex B2B concepts.

**Eye Contact Discipline**: Maintain consistent eye contact with camera lens, not the preview monitor. This creates the impression of direct conversation with the viewer and builds trust.

**Slide Timing Coordination**: Advance slides every 15-30 seconds, coordinated with script sections. Never change slides mid-sentence. Brief pauses during transitions feel natural and professional.

### Quality Assurance Standards

**Multiple Take Strategy**: Record multiple complete takes rather than stopping for minor mistakes. Label each take clearly (Take 1, Take 2, etc.) and review all options before selecting the best performance.

**Immediate Review Protocol**: Watch recordings immediately while the session is fresh in memory. Note timestamps of excellent moments and areas for improvement. This feedback informs future recordings.

**Technical Verification**: Test all technical elements (audio levels, camera focus, slide advancement) before beginning actual recording. Create a pre-recording checklist and follow it religiously.

**Backup and Organization**: Implement systematic file naming and backup procedures. Lost recordings represent significant time investment and cannot be easily recreated with the same energy and performance.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Strategic Planning Errors

**Skipping Creative Brief Development**: Jumping directly to recording without thorough avatar research and messaging strategy. This leads to unfocused content that doesn't resonate with prospects or address their specific pain points effectively.

**Script Over-Complexity**: Writing scripts that are too long, too detailed, or too formal. VSLs should feel conversational and natural, not like academic presentations. Aim for 1,200-1,500 words maximum for 5-10 minute videos.

**Proof Stack Weakness**: Using vague testimonials or case studies without specific metrics. "Great results" means nothing to sophisticated B2B buyers. Include exact numbers, timeframes, and company contexts.

**Congruency Breaks**: Misaligning VSL messaging with ad copy or landing page content. Even small disconnects create confusion and reduce trust, significantly impacting conversion rates.

### Technical Production Mistakes

**Audio Neglect**: Prioritizing video quality over audio quality. Poor audio immediately signals amateur production and causes prospects to question your competence. Invest in audio first, video second.

**Lighting Inconsistency**: Using natural light that changes throughout recording or mixing different color temperatures. This creates unprofessional appearance and distracts from message delivery.

**Improper Camera Positioning**: Shooting from unflattering angles (too low, too high, too far) or poor framing (too tight, too loose, off-center). Camera position significantly impacts perceived authority and credibility.

**OBS Configuration Errors**: Using default settings without optimization for VSL recording. Incorrect encoding settings lead to poor quality or unnecessarily large files that are difficult to upload and stream.

### Recording Performance Issues

**Energy Mismatch**: Speaking with normal conversational energy instead of compensating for camera energy absorption. Results in flat, unengaging delivery that fails to hold prospect attention.

**Script Dependence**: Reading directly from script instead of using it as a guide. This creates unnatural delivery and breaks eye contact with camera, reducing personal connection.

**Perfectionism Paralysis**: Stopping recording for minor mistakes instead of completing full takes. This breaks flow and energy, often resulting in worse overall performance than accepting minor imperfections.

**Slide Timing Problems**: Advancing slides too quickly, too slowly, or at awkward moments (mid-sentence). Poor timing disrupts flow and makes content harder to follow.

### Post-Production and Management Errors

**Immediate Review Skipping**: Not watching recordings immediately after completion. Memory of performance quality fades quickly, making it difficult to evaluate and improve future recordings.

**File Organization Neglect**: Poor naming conventions and backup procedures. Lost recordings represent significant time investment that cannot be easily recreated with the same performance quality.

**Quality Compromise**: Accepting poor recordings instead of re-shooting when quality doesn't meet standards. VSLs are long-term assets that should represent your best work, not quick compromises.

**Iteration Avoidance**: Treating first recording as final instead of testing and improving based on performance data. VSL optimization is an ongoing process that significantly impacts conversion rates.
